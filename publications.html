Selected Publications

The 'neat' and 'messy' in task-dependent neural geometry and computation
Xue C, Okazawa G
Trends in Neurosciences (2026, in press)
We propose a framework distinguishing "neat" (low-dimensional, structured) and "messy" (high-dimensional, entangled) neural geometry, and discuss how task demands shape which regime a circuit operates in — with implications for computation and readout.
PDF
Limitation of switching sensory information flow in flexible perceptual decision making
Luo T, Xu M, Zheng Z, Okazawa G
Nature Communications 16:172 (2025)
Immediately after switching a task rule, there is a brief reduction in the efficiency of sensory evidence processing, pinpointing a computational bottleneck in flexible decision-making circuits.
Paper PDF
Representational geometry of perceptual decisions in the monkey parietal cortex
Okazawa G, Hatch CE, Mancoo A, Machens CK, Kiani R
Cell 184:3748–3761 (2021)
Sensory evidence for perceptual decisions is encoded along a non-linear, task-dependent manifold in parietal neural state space — not as the average firing rate of individual neurons as previously assumed.
Paper PDF Code
Image statistics underlying natural texture selectivity of neurons in macaque V4
Okazawa G, Tajima S, Komatsu H
Proc Natl Acad Sci USA 112(4):E351–E360 (2015)
Single V4 neurons respond to naturalistic textures and these responses can be explained as selectivity for higher-order image statistics.
